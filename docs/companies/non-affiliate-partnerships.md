# Partnerships when there is no official affiliate programme

A practical playbook for publishers (e.g. Netherlands-focused expat guides) who want **measurable or commercial** relationships with **banks, insurers, relocators, tax firms, schools, telcos, and utilities** that **do not** list an open **Awin / Daisycon / Impact / in-house affiliate** programme.

**Companion docs:** [05-complementary-products.md](../backlinks/05-complementary-products.md) (categories and pitch hooks), [target-listings-by-category.md](./target-listings-by-category.md) (who is B2B vs affiliate), [discovery-automation.md](../backlinks/discovery-automation.md) (research workflow), [shared-templates.md](../backlinks/outreaches/shared-templates.md) (placeholders and variants).

**Disclaimer:** This is **operational and educational** guidance, not legal advice. **EU consumer law** and **your editorial policy** govern disclosures. Regulated sectors (financial services, health, legal) often require **compliance review** on their side; expect slow answers and **no** performance marketing in some cases.

---

## 1. Why many relevant brands have “no affiliate programme”

| Reason | What it means for you |
|--------|----------------------|
| **B2B or high-trust sales** | Relocation, immigration law, tax, corporate mobility: revenue is **contracts and retainers**, not publisher CPAs. |
| **Compliance & brand** | Banks and insurers restrict **third-party incentives**; they may still do **education**, **PR**, or **controlled** partnerships. |
| **Geography** | A brand may run affiliates in one country but **not** in NL; the local team may still partner on **content** or **events**. |
| **Capacity** | Smaller firms have no partnerships inbox; the **owner or marketing lead** decides. |
| **Affiliate exists but is hidden** | Programme is **invite-only** or only on a **specific network** after signup. Worth asking: “Do you work with publishers under NDA or via agency?” |

**Implication:** Your default ask should not be “join my affiliate network.” Lead with **audience fit**, **editorial quality**, and a **clear, low-friction** first step.

## 2. A ladder of engagement (start low, escalate with proof)

Use this sequence so you do not over-ask before you have **traffic**, **trust**, or **examples**.

1. **Editorial mention** — You cite them neutrally because they are relevant; **no** payment. Builds a relationship and shows professionalism.
2. **Resource swap** — They link your guide from a FAQ or blog; you already mention them fairly. Still often **unpaid**.
3. **Co-marketing** — Joint checklist, PDF, webinar, or newsletter feature. May be **unpaid** or **small fee**.
4. **Sponsored placement** — Fixed fee for a **labelled** block or series; contract + disclosure.
5. **Custom performance deal** — **Per lead**, **per appointment**, or **revenue share** agreed **bilaterally** (not via a network).
6. **Formal introducer / referrer** — Common in **B2B** (relocation, tax): they pay **you** or **credit** for referred clients under a **written** agreement.

You can skip steps if you already have a warm contact, but **cold outreach** rarely opens at step 5 or 6 without prior context.

## 3. Package types (what you actually sell or trade)

### 3.1 Editorial and “official resource”

**What:** Your page is listed as an **external** “read this first” link on their site, in-app copy, or onboarding email.

**Commercial:** Usually **free**; sometimes a **one-off fee** if they want exclusivity or heavy co-editing.

**Fit:** Universities, expat centres, employers’ relocation pages, housing platforms’ help centres.

---

### 3.2 Co-branded asset

**What:** Downloadable **checklist**, **timeline**, or **FAQ** with both logos; hosted on your site or theirs; single canonical URL.

**Commercial:** **Flat project fee**, or **free** if they promote it heavily to a large list.

**Fit:** Banks (strict compliance), municipalities’ international desks, large relocators.

---

### 3.3 Newsletter or community slot

**What:** One feature in their **email**, **Slack**, **LinkedIn**, or **customer** community.

**Commercial:** **Flat fee** or **trade** (they promote you; you mention them in a relevant guide).

**Fit:** Coworking spaces, recruiter newsletters, expat associations.

---

### 3.4 Sponsored content block (fixed fee)

**What:** Clearly **labelled** “Partnership” or “Supported by” module on a **specific** URL; copy may be **you-written** and **their-legal-approved**.

**Commercial:** **CPM-equivalent** or **flat monthly** for a slot; **not** tied to a network pixel.

**Fit:** Brands that refuse “affiliate” language but accept **marketing budget**.

---

### 3.5 Custom performance (bespoke CPA / CPL)

**What:** You send traffic to a **dedicated** URL or they issue a **promo code**; they report **conversions** monthly (or you agree on a **self-serve** dashboard).

**Commercial:** **Per qualified lead**, **per booked call**, **per activated account** (define **qualified** in writing).

**Fit:** Tax firms, visa consultancies, mid-size insurers’ **broker** desks, relocation agencies.

**Risk:** **Attribution disputes** without good tracking—see §6.

---

### 3.6 Referral and introducer (B2B)

**What:** You introduce readers who **opt in** (“request an intro” form); their sales team closes.

**Commercial:** **Flat finder’s fee**, **percentage of first invoice**, or **reciprocal** referrals.

**Fit:** Lawyers, accountants, payroll/umbrella, corporate relocation.

---

### 3.7 Events and workshops

**What:** You speak or host; they sponsor room or list; **recap post** links to your site.

**Commercial:** Often **unpaid** exposure; sometimes **speaker fee** or **sponsor** package.

**Fit:** Chambers of commerce, coworking, universities.

## 4. How the relationship works (operational checklist)

| Stage | Your actions | Their typical actions |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Qualify** | Confirm ICP: do they serve **your** readers (expats, English, NL-specific)? | — |
| **Find owner** | Marketing, partnerships, “growth”, or **founder** for SMB. | Route from generic inbox. |
| **First ask** | **One** concrete asset + **one** CTA (e.g. “link from your FAQ”). | Legal / compliance on regulated brands. |
| **Pilot** | **90-day** trial with **defined** URLs and reporting. | Analytics or CRM export. |
| **Contract** | For money or data-sharing: **scope, disclosure, termination, DPA** if needed. | Procurement, template MSA. |
| **Review** | Quarterly: clicks, leads, complaints, content drift. | Renew or adjust. |

**Regulated sectors:** Assume **no** customised tracking scripts on your page until they confirm. Prefer **their** landing URL + **UTM** + **their** disclosure text.

## 5. Commercial shapes (without an affiliate network)

| Model | When to use | Watch-outs |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Flat fee** | Sponsored block, newsletter, co-branded PDF | Cap revisions; define placement and duration. |
| **CPL (cost per lead)** | Form fills or “book a call” from a **tagged** landing page | Define **valid** lead (country, intent, not duplicate). |
| **CPA (cost per acquisition)** | Account opened, policy bound, contract signed | Long lag; **fraud** and **cancellation** clauses. |
| **Revenue share** | Rare without trust; sometimes **umbrella** or **recurring** services | Need visibility into **downstream** revenue or honour system. |
| **Hybrid** | Small retainer + performance kicker | Good for testing. |
| **Barter** | Cross-promotion only | Still **disclose** if material; tax treatment varies. |

Always specify: **currency**, **VAT**, **payment terms**, **reporting cadence**, and **who** is the counterparty (NL entity vs parent).

## 6. Tracking and attribution without a network

Networks exist partly to **attribute** and **settle**. Without them, you rebuild a **minimum viable** stack.

### 6.1 UTM discipline (baseline)

Use a **fixed** taxonomy, for example:

`utm_source=expatcopilot&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=reloc-{{brand_slug}}&utm_content={{page_slug}}`

Put UTMs on **every** outbound link you control. Store the **canonical** partner URL in a spreadsheet with **effective dates** so old content does not silently break.

### 6.2 Dedicated landing paths

Ask for a **path** only for your traffic, e.g. `brand.com/expatcopilot` or `brand.com/partners/expat-guides`, redirecting to the right product page. Easier for them to segment in **analytics**.

### 6.3 Promo or referral codes

They issue **`EXPCOPILOT`** (or similar) for **quotes** or checkout. You disclose the code’s nature in copy. **Limitation:** Under-reporting if users forget the code.

### 6.4 Post-conversion question

“How did you hear about us?” with **your brand** as an option. Works for **calls** and **intake forms**; depends on their **CRM** discipline.

### 6.5 Periodic reporting

**Monthly** email: clicks from your **GA4** (event `outbound_link_click` — see **§6.7**), **their** leads or signups attributed to your UTMs or path. Disputes go to a **named** contact.

### 6.6 What to avoid without legal sign-off

- Pixels that **drop cookies** on their site from your ad account.
- **Scraping** their CRM or pretending to be the user.
- **Incentivised** clicks (violates many programmes and some laws).

### 6.7 How we track outbound clicks on Expat Copilot (implementation)

This stack answers: **“How many times did readers leave our site to Brand X, from which page?”** It complements **UTMs on the `href`** (§6.1): UTMs help **their** analytics; **your** events prove **your** traffic for partnership reporting.

#### Behaviour (product)

1. On **click** of an external link, the app sends a **single custom event** with the **destination URL**, **link type**, **page path**, and optional **link text**.
2. Firing is **consent-aware**: if the user has not consented to analytics, **no** event is sent (`canSendAnalyticsEvents()` / GA-specific gates in code).
3. The same payload is sent to **Google Analytics 4** (direct `gtag` or **GTM `dataLayer`**, depending on config) and, when enabled, **PostHog**.

#### Code paths (repo)

| Piece | Role |
|-------|------|
| [`apps/expatlife-web/lib/analytics/track.ts`](../../apps/expatlife-web/lib/analytics/track.ts) | `trackOutboundLink({ destination_url, link_text, page_context, link_type })` → GA `trackEvent("outbound_link_click", …)` + PostHog `outbound_link_click` |
| [`apps/expatlife-web/lib/analytics/ga.ts`](../../apps/expatlife-web/lib/analytics/ga.ts) | `trackEvent` pushes `{ event: name, …params }` to `dataLayer` (GTM) or `gtag("event", …)` (direct GA4) |
| [`apps/expatlife-web/components/analytics/TrackedExternalLink.tsx`](../../apps/expatlife-web/components/analytics/TrackedExternalLink.tsx) | Client component: wraps `<a href>`, calls `trackOutboundLink` in `onClick` |

`link_type` is typed as `OutboundLinkType`: `"provider"` | `"official_source"` | `"external_resource"` — use **`provider`** for commercial / directory outbound; **`official_source`** for government or authority sites; **`external_resource`** for general editorial links.

#### Example A — tracked external link (React)

```tsx
import { TrackedExternalLink } from "@/components/analytics/TrackedExternalLink";

<TrackedExternalLink
  href="https://partner.example/get-started?utm_source=expatcopilot&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=reloc-partner"
  linkType="provider"
  linkText="Get a quote"
  rel="noopener noreferrer"
  target="_blank"
>
  Get a quote
</TrackedExternalLink>
```

`page_context` defaults to the **current pathname** from `usePathname()`; override if the link lives in a modal or shared layout where the pathname is misleading.

#### Example B — imperative call (e.g. custom card or MDX wrapper)

```tsx
import { trackOutboundLink } from "@/lib/analytics/track";

function onPartnerClick(href: string) {
  trackOutboundLink({
    destination_url: href,
    link_text: "Fragomen — immigration",
    page_context: "/netherlands/services/immigration-lawyers/",
    link_type: "provider",
  });
}
```

Call **before** navigation (e.g. in the same `onClick` as opening the URL). The browser will still follow `href` on a normal left-click.

#### Example C — what lands in GA4 / GTM

With GTM, `trackEvent` does effectively:

```js
dataLayer.push({
  event: "outbound_link_click",
  destination_url: "https://…",
  link_text: "…",
  page_context: "/netherlands/…/",
  link_type: "provider",
});
```

In **GA4 → Explore**, build a table or funnel where **Event name** = `outbound_link_click` and break down by `destination_url`, `link_type`, or `page_context`. For **partner reports**, export or screenshot counts for a date range + filter `destination_url` contains `partner-domain`.

#### Example D — strengthening the payload for B2B deals (optional product work)

For clearer **per-partner** reporting without relying only on URL substrings, you can extend the event (requires a small code change): e.g. add `partner_slug`, `campaign`, or `placement` to `OutboundLinkParams` and pass them from provider data. Keep **PII out** of analytics params.

#### Limits (be honest with partners)

| Limit | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| **Consent** | Users who reject analytics are invisible in your counts; **undercount** vs server logs. |
| **Ad blockers** | Same — client-side events may never fire. |
| **Middle-click / “open in new tab”** | Behaviour depends on browser; most paths still run `onClick` for a plain `<a>`, but do not rely on 100% parity with server-side click logs. |
| **Not a conversion** | This is **click-out**, not **lead** or **sale**; pair with their **UTM** or **CRM** for downstream proof (§6.2–6.4). |

#### Server-side alternative (if you need closer to “truth”)

If you later add **link redirects** on your own domain (e.g. `/go/partner?…` → 302 to partner), you can log **server-side** hits and avoid some blocker loss — at the cost of URL hygiene and maintenance. The current app pattern is **client events + UTM on destination URLs**.

## 7. What you offer them (value exchange)

Be explicit. Publishers often under-sell **non-cash** value.

| Asset | Why it matters to a B2B brand |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| **Qualified intent** | Your reader is often **mid-funnel** (visa approved, move date, employer letter). |
| **English-first context** | Reduces support tickets if your guide matches their **process** wording. |
| **SEO and long-tail** | You rank for **questions** their corporate site does not target. |
| **Trust transfer** | Editorial tone can feel **less salesy** than their landing page alone. |
| **Feedback loop** | You can surface **FAQ gaps** (what readers misunderstand about their product). |
| **Co-created proof** | Case-style **“first 30 days”** flows with their **compliance-approved** facts. |

Lead with **one** of these in the first email, not with your **traffic** graph unless it is impressive.

## 8. Outreach approach (process)

1. **Consume their material** — Read the exact **FAQ**, **PDF**, or **product** page you complement. Quote it once in the email (proves you did the work).
2. **Pick one URL** — Your **best** guide for **their** audience segment; avoid sending five links.
3. **Lowest reasonable ask** — e.g. “Worth a line in your international FAQ?” before “Can we do CPL?”
4. **Clear unsubscribe from your side** — “If this is the wrong team, who owns international content?”
5. **Follow-up** — **7–10 business days**, **one** bump; third touch only for **high-value** targets.
6. **Log everything** — Date, contact, URL offered, response, next step (see [discovery-automation.md](../backlinks/discovery-automation.md)).

## 10. Disclosure and editorial policy (non-negotiable)

- **Paid** placement, **sponsored** module, **referral** fee, or **performance** deal that influences **ranking** or **placement** must be **clear to readers** (site-wide disclosure page + **per-page** labelling where material).
- **Pure editorial** links with **no** compensation still benefit from a **general** affiliate/partnership policy so readers understand when money **does** change hands elsewhere.
- Do not imply **government** or **legal** endorsement. For **tax and legal** topics, keep **non-advice** language and link to **official** sources.

Align with your live pages: e.g. `/affiliate-disclosure/`, `/editorial-policy/`.

---

## 11. When to walk away

- They want **undisclosed** paid placement in **comparison** or **“best of”** content you present as independent ranking.
- They require **factually wrong** claims or **guaranteed** outcomes (visas, tax, insurance eligibility).
- **Reporting** is opaque and they still want **performance** pricing.
- **Exclusivity** that blocks you from mentioning **alternatives** readers need.

## 12. Quick reference — mapping package to tracking

| Package | Primary tracking | Backup |
|---------|------------------|--------|
| Editorial link | UTM on your outbound link | Their referrer report |
| Clicks from your guides / directories | GA4 / PostHog **`outbound_link_click`** (see **§6.7**) + UTMs on `href` | Partner’s UTM reports |
| Co-branded PDF | QR or short URL with UTM | Download hosting stats |
| Newsletter | Unique link per send | Click report from their ESP |
| Sponsored block | UTM + contract dates | Screenshot of placement |
| CPL / CPA | Dedicated URL + CRM field | Monthly export |
| Referral intro | Intro log + spreadsheet | Their sales confirmation |
