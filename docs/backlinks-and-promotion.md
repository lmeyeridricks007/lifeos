# Backlinks, promotion, and automation (ExpatLife / ExpatCopilot)

This document is a **practical playbook** for earning links and traffic to the site: what to aim for, where to show up, how to measure progress, and **what you can automate** without crossing into spam or policy violations.

**Principles (read first)**

- **Links follow reputation.** The durable path is useful tools, guides, and data that others *choose* to cite.
- **Google’s view:** Natural, editorial links from relevant sites matter; manipulative schemes (paid link networks, mass low-quality directories, comment spam) risk penalties. When in doubt, ask: “Would I still want this link if SEO did not exist?”
- **Dutch / expat context:** Prioritize **English and Dutch** publishers, **relocation and immigration** professionals, **employers and HR**, **universities**, **expat communities**, and **complementary services** (housing, tax, insurance) where a link helps their audience.

---

## 1. What “good” backlinks look like

| Signal | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| **Topical relevance** | A link from a relocation lawyer, university international office, or expat blog fits the site’s topic. |
| **Editorial context** | The link appears inside an article or resource list with a reason, not a footer “partners” dump of 50 URLs. |
| **Traffic & trust** | The referring page gets real visits or belongs to a recognized institution. |
| **Dofollow vs nofollow** | Dofollow passes more traditional “PageRank-style” signal; **nofollow** still drives **referral traffic** and **discovery**. Pursue both. |
| **Anchor text** | Mostly **natural** (brand name, URL, “this guide”). Avoid over-optimizing exact-match anchors (“best Netherlands visa lawyer Amsterdam”) at scale. |

**Tier your efforts:** Tier 1 = a handful of **high-trust** links (university, government-adjacent resource pages, major media). Tier 2 = **industry** (relocation, HR, legal). Tier 3 = **communities** (forums, newsletters, podcasts). Tier 4 = **directories and lists** (use sparingly; many are low value).

### 2.1 Institutions and “.edu” / official-adjacent pages

- **University international offices** — student arrival checklists, housing, visa overviews. Offer a **specific guide** or **tool** (e.g. checklist, timeline) that fills a gap on their page.
- **Embassy / consulate** — some link to **third-party** resources; policies vary; always pitch **factual, non-commercial** angles.
- **Municipal expat pages** (gemeente “internationals” sections) — often curate links; local relevance is strong.

**How to approach:** Short email to the **web editor or international desk**: what page you found, what’s missing or outdated, one URL to your resource, no pressure.

### 2.2 Professional services (relocation, immigration law, tax)

- **Relocation agencies and immigration lawyers** often maintain “resources” or blog posts. **Win-win:** guest explainer (unique content) or **co-branded** checklist with clear authorship and a single canonical link to your site.
- **HR / employer resources** — companies hiring from abroad need **checklists**. Target **employer branding / talent acquisition** contacts.

### 2.3 Media and PR

- **Journalists** covering mobility, tech talent in NL, housing crises, etc. Use **HARO**-style services (see automation) or direct pitches with **data** from your content (if you have original stats or surveys later).

### 2.4 Communities and UGC (use carefully)

- **Reddit** (r/Netherlands, r/Amsterdam, country-specific subs), **Facebook groups**, **Slack/Discord** expat servers — participate first; link **only** when it directly answers a question (community rules vary; many ban self-promotion).
- **Expat blogs and newsletters** — offer **quotes**, **expert tips**, or **exclusive mini-guides** in exchange for attribution + link.

### 2.5 Complementary products

- **Housing platforms, banks, insurers, accountants** — partnership pages, “moving to NL” hubs, affiliate or **editorial** placements (disclose commercial relationships where required).

### 2.6 Directories and roundups

- **High-quality** niche lists (e.g. “best expat resources for the Netherlands”) can be worth a submission. **Avoid** bulk submission to hundreds of generic SEO directories.

### 2.7 Your own levers (on-site)

- **Original tools and calculators** attract **natural** links if they’re embeddable or clearly useful (with shareable URLs).
- **Methodology / editorial policy** pages build trust when outreach targets check the site.
- **Press / media kit** page (optional): logo, angles, contact — reduces friction for journalists.

## 3. Where to promote the site (beyond SEO)

**Full playbook** (what to include per channel, **cadence / how often**, measurement, and channels **deliberately excluded** as poor website drivers): **[`docs/backlinks/promotions/where-to-promote-beyond-seo.md`](./backlinks/promotions/where-to-promote-beyond-seo.md)** · [index](./backlinks/promotions/README.md).

| Channel | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Organic search** | Long-term; backlinks + content quality + technical SEO help. *(Parallel track—not “promotion” in the sense of §3.)* |
| **Newsletters** | Yours (if you build one) or **sponsorship** / mentions in expat newsletters. |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B angles: employers, HR, relocation; **thought leadership** snippets linking to deep guides. |
| **X (Twitter) / Threads** | Short threads + link to **one** strong article; engage with NL expat accounts. |
| **YouTube / short video** | “First week in NL” style with link in description (high effort, high trust). |
| **Podcasts** | Guest spots on **expat** or **immigration** shows; show notes almost always include a link. |
| **Meetups & events** | Amsterdam expat events, startup weeks — QR or short URL on slides. |
| **Paid** | Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn — **not backlinks**, but **distribution**; use for testing messaging and landing pages. |

Match **one primary CTA** per campaign (e.g. a single pillar guide or tool), not the homepage every time.

## 4. Process: repeatable workflow (manual core)

1. **Asset inventory** — List **10–20** pages/tools worth promoting (best depth, unique angle).
2. **Prospect list** — For each asset, 20–50 targets (URLs of pages that could logically link).
3. **Gap analysis** — Note what’s broken, thin, or missing on *their* page (your email hook).
4. **Outreach** — Personalized, short, one ask. Track status (see automation).
5. **Follow-up** — One polite follow-up after ~7–10 days; then stop.
6. **Measure** — New referring domains, traffic from referral, rankings for target queries (Search Console).

## 5. Automation and semi-automation

Automation should support **research, monitoring, and workflow**—not **mass unsolicited spam**. Use tools in line with their terms of service and **GDPR** where you store contact data.

### 5.1 Monitoring new links and mentions (high value, low risk)

- **Google Search Console** — “Links” report: top linking sites, top linked pages. Manual but free.
- **Alerts**
  - **Google Alerts** or **Talkwalker** / **Awario** for brand name + domain.
  - **RSS** from competitor **backlink tools’** exports (if you subscribe) or from your own monthly export.
- **Automate:** Scheduled email (e.g. weekly digest) when a script compares this week’s GSC export to last week’s (store CSVs in Drive/S3; diff in Python or Google Apps Script).

### 5.2 Prospecting (semi-automated)

- **Search operators** (manual or scripted):  
  `site:.nl "moving to the Netherlands"`, `"expat" "resources"`, etc.
- **APIs** (paid): Ahrefs, Semrush, Moz — pull **backlinks to competitors** or **pages that link to** a known resource, then filter in a spreadsheet.
- **Automate:** Python/notebook that ingests CSV export, dedupes domains, scores by simple rules (TLD, keyword in title, DR if available).

### 5.3 Outreach tracking (CRM-lite)

- **Airtable / Notion / Google Sheet** with columns: URL, contact, status, date contacted, asset pitched, response.
- **Automate:** Zapier/Make: when a row is set to “Won,” post to Slack; or when a blog post is published (RSS), create draft rows for “syndication targets.”

### 5.4 Email sequences (use with restraint)

- **Personalized first line** should never be fully automated fake personalization.
- **Automate:** Template **blocks** (signature, asset links), mail merge for **name** and **their page URL** only if verified.
- Tools: **Instantly**, **lemlist**, **HubSpot** sequences — cap volume; prefer **warm** lists over cold spray.

### 5.5 HARO / journalist requests (semi-automated)

- Services like **HARO**, **Qwoted**, **SourceBottle** send journalist requests by email.
- **Automate:** Filter emails by keywords (`Netherlands`, `expat`, `visa`, `relocation`) into a folder; optional script to summarize daily digest. **Response quality** stays manual.

### 5.6 Social distribution

- **Buffer**, **Hypefury**, **Typefully** — queue posts linking to **new** or **evergreen** guides on a schedule.
- **Automate:** CI or Zapier on **RSS** (if you publish an RSS feed from the blog) → draft social posts for human approval.

### 5.7 Content syndication (careful)

- **Medium**, **LinkedIn articles** — can duplicate content; use **canonical** link back to the site or **summary + link** to avoid SEO duplication issues. Automate cross-posting only with a clear canonical strategy.

### 5.8 What not to “automate”

- Mass blog comments, forum spam, or **paid link packs**.
- **Scraped** emails blasted without opt-in (legal and reputational risk).
- **AI-generated** outreach at scale without human review (reads as spam quickly).

## 6. Metrics and review cadence

| Metric | Tool | Cadence |
|--------|------|---------|
| Referring domains / new backlinks | Search Console, Ahrefs/Semrush | Monthly |
| Referral traffic | GA4 | Monthly |
| Rankings for target keywords | GSC, rank trackers | Monthly |
| Conversion (newsletter, tool usage) | GA4, product analytics | Quarterly |

Set **one numeric goal** per quarter (e.g. “+5 relevant referring domains” or “one .edu link”) rather than chasing raw link count.

---

## 7. Checklist: new guide or tool launch

- [ ] **On-page:** Title, meta description, canonical URL, internal links from related guides.
- [ ] **Sitemap:** Confirm route is public (`publish` / `publishDate` if using scheduled publishing).
- [ ] **One “hero” outreach list:** 15–30 highly relevant pages to pitch or comment (where allowed).
- [ ] **One social thread** + **one newsletter** mention (if applicable).
- [ ] **Monitor** GSC for impressions/clicks in the first 4–6 weeks.
