## 1. Principles (read before you DM)

- **Comment or react before you pitch** when possible. A thoughtful comment on their post beats a cold “I’d love to connect” from nowhere.
- **One clear ask per thread:** a single guide or tool URL with UTM—not five links and a calendar link.
- **Disclose lightly:** “I run Expat Copilot (editorial NL guides)” so it’s obvious you’re not posing as a lawyer or government.
- **Respect platform limits:** LinkedIn restricts automation and bulk outreach; keep volume **human-scale** (see [§15](#15-volume-guardrails-and-troubleshooting)).
- **No medical/legal advice** in messages; frame everything as **practical orientation** and link to your **editorial policy** if challenged.

**UTM pattern (use consistently):**  
`https://www.expatcopilot.com/netherlands/PATH/?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=dm&utm_campaign=SHORT_LABEL`

| `utm_campaign` examples | Use when |
|---------------------------|----------|
| `uni-intl` | University international office |
| `hr-mobility` | HR / people advisory |
| `recruiter-hsm` | Agency recruiter |
| `legal-peer` | Law / relocation firm |
| `press` | Journalist |
| `partner-housing` | Housing / complementary brand |
| `post-thread-visa` | Traffic from a specific post (match post topic) |

## 2. Profile setup (step-by-step)

Do this **before** you send connection notes so acceptances and replies go up.

### Step 1 — Open your profile in edit mode

1. Go to your LinkedIn profile.  
2. Click **Add profile section** or the **pencil** on each block you need to fix.  
3. Work top to bottom: photo → banner → headline → About → Featured → Experience.

### Step 2 — Photo & banner (trust)

- **Photo:** clear face, neutral background; same as you use for press if possible.  
- **Banner (optional but strong):** simple text *“Netherlands relocation guides & tools · Expat Copilot”* or a calm NL-relevant image—avoid cluttered stock.

### Step 3 — Headline (one line, searchable)

**Formula:** `{Outcome you deliver} · {Site/brand} · {Credibility hook}`

**Examples:**

- `Practical guides for moving to the Netherlands · Expat Copilot · SA → NL`
- `Netherlands arrival admin, visas & tools (editorial) · expatcopilot.com`

Avoid vague titles like “Passionate about helping people” with no topic.

### Step 4 — About (3–6 short paragraphs)

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Line 1–2:** Who you help (“internationals and HR teams dealing with NL admin”).  
2. **Line 3–4:** Your proof (“I moved from South Africa to the Netherlands and…” ).  
3. **Line 5:** What Expat Copilot is (**editorial**, not legal/tax advice).  
4. **Line 6:** **One** link: `https://www.expatcopilot.com`  
5. Optional: “Open to being a **source** for journalists on NL mobility topics.”

**Example About (adapt, don’t copy verbatim if you want your own voice):**

> I help people cut through the noise of moving to the Netherlands—gemeente registration, BSN, insurance, visas—using clear checklists and tools.  
>  
> I moved from South Africa to the Netherlands and learned most of this the hard way first.  
>  
> **Expat Copilot** is editorial content only: practical guides and calculators, not immigration or tax advice.  
>  
> https://www.expatcopilot.com  
>  
> Happy to connect with student services, HR, recruiters, and comms teams who need neutral “first 90 days” context for their audiences.

### Step 5 — Featured (2–3 links)

1. On profile, **Add featured** → **Link**.  
2. Add, for example:  
   - **Compare visas** or **After arriving** (pillar)  
   - **One flagship tool** (e.g. visa checker or checklist)  
   - **Editorial policy** or **Methodology** (trust)  
3. Use **custom titles** so it’s obvious what each link is (not “expatcopilot.com” x3).

### Step 6 — Experience

1. **Add position:** Expat Copilot · Founder / Editor (or your title).  
2. **Description:** 2–3 bullets—topics you cover (visas, housing admin, insurance orientation).  
3. If you have **prior** relevant jobs, keep them; they increase trust for HR/university audiences.

### Step 7 — Contact info

1. **Contact info** → add **Website** (company URL).  
2. If you use a **public email** for partnerships, add it; many university staff prefer email over LinkedIn long-term.

### Step 8 — Creator mode (optional)

If you post regularly: enable **Creator mode** and pick **5 hashtags** you actually use (e.g. `#Netherlands`, `#Expat`, `#Relocation`, `#HR`, `#Immigration`—only if you’ll post on them). Skip if you won’t maintain consistency.

## 3. Find the right people (step-by-step)

### Step 1 — Define today’s micro-segment

Pick **one** audience for this session (better than mixing everyone):

- International student services / university  
- HR / mobility / people advisory  
- Recruiters (tech / international)  
- Immigration or relocation **marketing** (not cold-DMing lawyers for legal work)  
- Housing / expat-service **content** leads  
- Journalists / editors  

### Step 2 — Search on LinkedIn

1. Click **Search** at the top.  
2. Type a **role + geography** string, e.g.  
   - `international student services Netherlands`  
   - `global mobility Amsterdam`  
   - `immigration Netherlands marketing`  
3. Press Enter → click **People**.  
4. Use filters: **Locations** → Netherlands (or city); **Current company** if you target one org.

**Search query ideas (rotate weekly):**

| Goal | Try searching |
|------|----------------|
| Universities | `international officer`, `student affairs`, `internationalisation` + city name |
| HR / mobility | `global mobility`, `relocations specialist`, `people advisory`, `talent Netherlands` |
| Recruiters | `technical recruiter Netherlands`, `expat recruitment` |
| Content / comms | `content marketing` + `relocation` / `expat` / `housing` |
| Press | `editor` + `Netherlands` + `expat` / `mobility` |

### Step 3 — Open 10 profiles; shortlist 3–5

For each profile, check:

- **Current role** matches your segment.  
- **Activity** in last 30 days (posts or comments)—easier to personalize.  
- **Not** a pure sales hunter for unrelated industries (avoid wasting connects).

### Step 4 — Capture hooks in your sheet

For each shortlisted person, write **one line** you’ll use in the connect note:

- Title of a **recent post** they wrote, or  
- A **programme** on their employer site you skimmed, or  
- **Mutual** connection / alma mater (only if real).

**Example hook lines (for your notes column):**

- “Post on HSM salary thresholds — 12 Mar”  
- “UU international welcome week carousel”  
- “Shared People Matters piece on assignee experience”

### Step 5 — Build a saved list (optional)

1. From search results, use **Save to list** (if available on your account) or export names to a **Google Sheet** with columns from [§14](#14-tracking-sheet-and-example-row).  
2. Never exceed your **weekly caps** ([§15](#15-volume-guardrails-and-troubleshooting))—a list of 200 people is for **months**, not one day.

## 4. Path A: Connect, note, then DM (step-by-step)

**Use when:** You have a **specific** reason to connect (role + hook).

### Phase 1 — Warm up (same day or day before)

1. Open their **recent activity** or **Posts**.  
2. If there’s a relevant post: click **Like** **or** leave **one** comment (3–5 sentences, adds value—agree, add stat, share a caveat).  
3. If they have **no** posts: skip to Phase 2 but make the connect note **role-specific** (name their department or programme if public).

### Phase 2 — Send connection request

1. Go to their profile → click **Connect**.  
2. If LinkedIn shows **Add a note**, choose it (always prefer note over blank request for cold outreach).  
3. Paste your **short note** ([§6](#6-connection-request-notes)); verify **under ~280 characters**.  
4. **Do not** put a URL in the note.  
5. Click **Send invitation**.

### Phase 3 — Log the action

In your sheet: **date sent**, **hook used**, **segment**, **planned asset URL** for DM.

### Phase 4 — When they accept (notification or email)

1. Within **24–72 hours**, open **Messaging**.  
2. Start a **new message** to them (they’re now 1st degree).  
3. Send **first DM** from [§7](#7-first-dm-after-they-accept-personalized)—**one link** with UTM.  
4. Log **DM1 date** in sheet.

### Phase 5 — If no reply in ~7–10 business days

1. Send **follow-up #1** ([§8](#8-follow-ups-and-when-they-reply)) with a **new angle** (not copy-paste of DM1).  
2. Log **DM2 date**.

### Phase 6 — Tier-1 only (optional second follow-up)

1. If target is **flagship** (major uni, big employer comms, national media) and still silent: send **final short bump** ([§8](#8-follow-ups-and-when-they-reply)).  
2. Then **stop**—preserves reputation.

### Phase 7 — If they reply

Use [§8](#8-follow-ups-and-when-they-reply) reply scripts; move outcome to **Replied** / **Meeting** / **Forwarded to colleague**.

## 5. Path B: VIP without connect first (step-by-step)

**Use when:** Big-brand comms, busy editors, or anyone where **Connect** feels too forward.

### Step 1 — Follow their profile

1. Click **Follow** (and **Notify** on if you want alerts for their posts).

### Step 2 — Engage twice before any pitch

1. **Comment meaningfully** on **two separate posts** over **1–2 weeks** (not same-day spam).  
2. Comments should stand alone as useful—**no** “check my site” in the comment.

### Step 3 — Try message without connect (if available)

1. Look for **Message** on profile.  
2. If it opens: you may have **Open Profile**, **Premium**, **InMail**, or **Group** overlap.  
3. Send a **shorter** first note: introduce + **offer to be a source** + **ask permission** to send one link—some prefer you to ask before dropping URL.

**Example “permission first” DM:**

> Hi {{FIRST}} — I’ve appreciated your coverage of {{HOOK}}. I’m Lee; I moved SA→NL and edit **Expat Copilot** (editorial NL guides, not legal advice).  
>  
> Would it be alright if I sent **one** link to a practical resource that might help as background for future mobility stories—no pitch beyond that?

If they say yes → send **one** UTM link in the next message.

### Step 4 — If Message is locked

1. Either wait until you have a **mutual** who can intro, or  
2. Use **InMail** sparingly ([§12](#12-inmail-and-open-profile-when-you-cannot-dm)), or  
3. Fall back to **email** from their press page (see [outreach docs](../outreaches/README.md)).

### 8.8 When they decline or ignore after follow-up

- **Do not** argue.  
- Optionally **like** their future posts to stay on radar.  
- **Revisit in 6–12 months** if they publish something highly aligned—fresh hook, not the same DM.

## 9. Warm DM after they engage on your post

**Trigger:** They **liked** or **commented** on your post.

**Step 1:** Wait **a few hours to 1 day** (not seconds—avoids feeling stalky).  
**Step 2:** Open message → reference **exact post topic**.  
**Step 3:** **One** UTM link to the deep page.

Hi {{FIRST}},  

Thanks for the {{like/comment}} on my post about {{TOPIC}} — appreciated.  

If it’s useful, the full checklist/tool lives here:  
{{URL_WITH_UTM}}  

Always happy for feedback if something’s unclear.  

Lee  

**If they asked a question in comments:** answer **in the thread** first; DM can add “I replied above—here’s also the direct link: …”

## 15. Volume guardrails and troubleshooting

### Suggested caps (manual)

| Activity | Suggested cap |
|----------|----------------|
| Connection requests **with notes** | **~10–20 / week**, spread Mon–Fri |
| First DMs after accept | **~10–25 / week** |
| Cold DMs (no connect) | **Minimal** |
| Follow-up #1 | For all who got DM1 |
| Follow-up #2 | **Tier-1 only** |

### If LinkedIn restricts invitations

1. **Stop** new connects for **1–2 weeks**.  
2. Withdraw **stale** pending invites (older than ~3–4 weeks).  
3. Focus on **comments + posts** only.  
4. Never use **sketchy automation** to bypass limits.

### If nobody accepts

- Improve **profile** ([§2](#2-profile-setup-step-by-step)).  
- Make notes **more specific** (generic notes get ignored).  
- Ensure you’re not targeting **inactive** accounts.

### If acceptance rate is high but zero replies

- DMs may be too long—cut to **4–6** short lines.  
- Try **permission-first** (“OK if I send one link?”) for senior targets.  
- Test a **different asset** (tool vs long guide).

## 16. What not to do

- **Wall of links** or Calendly in the **first** message.  
- **Fake personalization** (“great post!” with no detail).  
- **Implying** you’re IND, a lawyer, or a government service.  
- **Automated** blast tools or duplicate DMs across dozens of people per hour.  
- **Demanding** links, replies, or meetings.  
- **Arguing** with mods, editors, or HR when they say no.
