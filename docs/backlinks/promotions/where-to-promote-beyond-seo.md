# Where to promote the site (beyond SEO)

**Audience:** Netherlands / expat relocation editorial (guides + tools).  
**Goal:** **Clicks and engaged visits** to https://www.expatcopilot.com (or your production domain)—not vanity impressions on platforms that rarely send website traffic.

**Companion:** [§2 backlinks & outreach](../README.md) (earning links); [main playbook §3](../../backlinks-and-promotion.md#3-where-to-promote-the-site-beyond-seo). This doc is **distribution**: you control the message and usually the link.

---

## How to use this doc

1. Pick **one primary URL per campaign** (a strong guide or tool—not always the homepage).  
2. Use **UTM parameters** on external links so GA4 shows which channel worked:  
   `?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=visa-checklist`  
3. Review **referral traffic + conversions** monthly; cut channels that don’t move numbers after a fair trial.

## Channels deliberately excluded (weak or risky for *website* engagement)

| Channel | Why omitted from the core plan |
|---------|--------------------------------|
| **Instagram feed** | Link friction; feed posts don’t send meaningful web traffic unless you already have scale + Story links. Not a first-tier web acquisition channel for this niche. |
| **TikTok** | Possible for awareness; **click-through to long-form guides** is usually low unless you build a dedicated funnel. Revisit only if you commit to native video volume. |
| **Pinterest** | Can work for **checklist / infographic** niches; only add if you produce **vertical visuals** and accept slow burn. Not default for text-heavy legal/visa content. |
| **Snapchat / Tumblr** | Poor fit for Netherlands expat research intent. |
| **Mass automated social blasting** | Low quality, policy risk, no durable clicks. |
| **Syndicating full articles to Medium/LinkedIn Articles** | Can **cannibalise** search if not handled with canonical/summary discipline; if used, prefer **short summary + single link** to the canonical URL on your domain only. |

## 1. Owned email newsletter

**Why it’s included:** Email routinely achieves the **highest click-through rates** of any owned channel; readers opted in to hear from you.

### What to include

- **One hero CTA** per send (one guide or tool URL with UTM). Secondary links: 1–2 max.  
- **Subject line:** specific outcome (“BSN registration: what to book first”) not “Monthly update.”  
- **Preview text:** complements subject; no bait-and-switch.  
- **Short personal framing** (e.g. one line from Lee’s SA → NL experience) + **scannable bullets** + single button/link block.  
- **Compliance:** clear signup source, easy unsubscribe, physical address if required by your ESP/jurisdiction.

### Cadence / how often

| Stage | Suggested frequency |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Launch / first 90 days** | **Biweekly** (every 2 weeks) so you don’t burn the list while you learn what gets clicks. |
| **Steady state (list engaged)** | **Weekly** if you have enough new or refreshed assets; otherwise stay biweekly. |
| **Quiet periods** | **Monthly minimum** so the list doesn’t go cold; send “best of” evergreen tools. |

**Rule of thumb:** never send **only** promotional noise; each issue should deliver **one clear takeaway** even if they don’t click.

### Measure

- Open rate (directional), **click rate**, clicks per link, **sessions from `utm_medium=email`** in GA4, optional tool completions.

## 2. Other people’s newsletters & cross-promos

**Why it’s included:** Borrowed audiences that already **click links** in email (expat, HR, mobility, city guides).

### What to include

- **One-line value prop** + **one URL** (tool or definitive guide) + optional 50-word blurb the host can paste.  
- **UTM:** `utm_source=newsletter_name&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=sponsor_or_swap`  
- **Assets:** square logo, author headshot, short bio, compliance line if content is educational not legal advice.

### Cadence / how often

| Type | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| **Paid sponsor slot** | **1–4× per year** per publication as tests; scale what shows referral traffic. |
| **Cross-promo / swap** | **Quarterly** with 1–2 aligned newsletters (similar audience, non-competing product). |
| **One-off mentions** | After **real relationships** (podcast host, event organiser)—not guaranteed; track manually. |

### Measure

Referral sessions from that domain in GA4; use unique UTMs per partner.

## 3. LinkedIn (personal + company)

**Why it’s included:** Links in posts and comments work; strong concentration of **employers, HR, recruiters, mobility, founders** who hire internationals in NL.

### What to include

- **Hook** (problem or myth) in **first 2 lines** before “see more.”  
- **Concrete takeaway** + **one link** to a guide/tool (not homepage every time).  
- Optional: **PDF carousel** with last slide = “full checklist on site” (drives profile visits; still push one URL in post body).  
- Tagging: use **sparingly**; prefer **meaningful** comments on others’ posts 3–5× weekly to stay visible without posting daily spam.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Publish post with link** | **2–4× per week** (personal); **1–2× per week** (company page, if you use one). |
| **Comment / engage (no link)** | **15–30 min/day** or **3–5 meaningful comments/day** on HR, mobility, NL hiring threads—builds reach for your next link post. |
| **Long-form “article” on LinkedIn** | **Avoid full syndication**; use **short post + link** to canonical on site. |

### Measure

Post analytics (impressions, clicks), GA4 `utm_source=linkedin`, DMs or inbound asks.

**Templates & DM workflow:** [linkedin-outreach-and-dms.md](./linkedin-outreach-and-dms.md).

## 4. Short-form microblogging (X, Threads, Bluesky)

**Why it’s included:** Netherlands, expat, Amsterdam, tech, and visa topics surface in real time; **one link per thread/post** can drive spiky referral traffic when a post catches.

### What to include

- **One thread or post = one asset** (visa comparison, checklist, calculator).  
- **Pin** a thread or profile link to your **best evergreen tool** if the platform allows.  
- **Context:** reply to news (IND changes, housing headlines) with **factual** framing + link—higher engagement than generic promo.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Posts with link** | **2–4× per week** per active platform (don’t duplicate robotically; adapt hook to platform tone). |
| **Replies / quote-posts without link** | **Daily light touch** where you add value—builds trust for the posts that do include a link. |

**Practical note:** Running **all three** (X, Threads, Bluesky) is optional; **pick one primary** unless you have bandwidth—quality beats empty cross-posting.

### Measure

Link clicks (platform analytics if available), GA4 referrals, follower growth is secondary to **clicks**.

## 5. Reddit & high-intent forums

**Why it’s included:** r/Netherlands, r/Amsterdam, country-specific subs, and some **expat forums** send **high-intent** visitors when your link **directly answers** a question.

### What to include

- **Answer in the comment** first; link as **“full step-by-step here”** only if rules allow.  
- **Disclose** if it’s your site (transparency reduces mod backlash).  
- **Deep links** (specific guide), never homepage-only.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Comments with a link** | **Only when it fits the question**—rough cap **2–6× per month** across subs if you’re doing it well (quality, not quota). |
| **Participation without links** | **Ongoing**—this is what makes occasional links acceptable. |

### Measure

Reddit → referral spikes in GA4; note which threads worked for future topics.

## 6. Facebook groups (selected)

**Why it’s included:** Many **large NL expat groups** are still where newcomers ask practical questions; **link posts** are often restricted—**comment replies** under your own helpful text work better.

**Full playbook + group directory:** [facebook-outreach-and-groups.md](./facebook-outreach-and-groups.md) (step-by-step, templates, **100+** group URLs to verify live).

**First 20 posts (full copy + calendar):** [facebook-starter-posts-20.md](./facebook-starter-posts-20.md).

### What to include

- Same discipline as Reddit: **value first**, one clear link, **disclose** affiliation.  
- **Screenshot or short written steps** in the post + link in first comment if group rules require it.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Link in context** | **2–4× per month** per group max if rules allow; many groups are stricter—**follow each group’s promo rules**. |
| **No-link help** | Regular presence **weekly** so you’re not a drive-by marketer. |

### Measure

`facebook.com` / `l.facebook.com` referrals in GA4; track which groups allow sustainable help+link.

## 7. Slack & Discord (expat & tech communities)

**Why it’s included:** Tech NL, international hires, and city channels often ask **visa, BSN, housing** questions; members **click** useful links in-thread.

### What to include

- **Search archives** before posting; drop link only if it **solves** the thread.  
- Introduce yourself in **#intros** where required; ask mods about **#resources** policies.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Links** | **As needed** when questions match—typically **a few per month** total across servers if you’re active. |
| **Presence** | **Weekly** check-in on 1–3 servers you care about—not 50 servers shallowly. |

### Measure

GA4 may show `discord.com` / `slack.com` or direct traffic after mentions; often **dark social**—also watch **branded search** bumps.

## 8. YouTube & Shorts

**Why it’s included:** **Description and pinned comment links** are standard; video builds **trust** for heavy topics (visa, insurance). High effort, but **strong click intent** from viewers who already invested time.

### What to include

- **Title + thumbnail** = one clear promise (not clickbait that contradicts the guide).  
- **Description:** link **above the fold** with UTM; **chapters** with timestamps; link to **one primary** guide/tool.  
- **Pinned comment:** same URL + disclaimer (educational, not legal advice).  
- **Shorts:** **Verbal CTA** (“link in description”) + same UTM link.

### Cadence / how often

| Format | Frequency (sustainable indie) |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| **Long-form (8–20 min)** | **1× per month** to start; **2×** if workflow is smooth. |
| **Shorts (30–60 sec)** | **2–4× per month** repurposing one insight per short, all pointing to **one** deep page per batch. |

### Measure

YouTube traffic sources → **external links** clicks; GA4 `utm_source=youtube`; average view duration.

## 9. Podcasts (as guest)

**Why it’s included:** **Show notes almost always include a link**; listeners often **open the site** while walking or after the episode—high-quality visits.

### What to include

- **One “home” URL** for the host (best tool or hub) + **2–3 deep links** you might mention aloud.  
- **Talking points** tied to **specific mistakes or checklists** (easy for listeners to remember and search).  
- After publish: **share clip** on LinkedIn/X with link to **episode + site**.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Guest spots** | **1–2 per quarter** as a realistic target at first; more if inbound picks up. |
| **Pitching** | **Ongoing pipeline**—batch 5 pitches/month to relevant shows (NL expat, European mobility, HR). |

### Measure

Referral from podcast domains, spike in **direct** and **branded search** after air date, UTM if host allows custom link in notes.

## 10. Meetups & events (NL hubs)

**Why it’s included:** **QR codes and short verbal URLs** convert attention in the room into **same-day traffic**; strong for tools (“everyone open this checklist”).

### What to include

- **One short path** or **vanity redirect** (e.g. `/amsterdam` or a featured tool) on slides.  
- **QR** to that URL with UTM `utm_source=event&utm_medium=offline&utm_campaign=meetup_name`.  
- **Printed one-pager** optional: one headline + QR + what the tool does.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Attend + mention site** | **1–2× per month** in Amsterdam/Rotterdam/The Hague/Eindhoven-style meetups if available. |
| **Speak / host** | **Quarterly** as a stretch goal—bigger spike. |

### Measure

UTM campaign hits same evening + day after; ask “how did you hear us” in optional on-site survey.

## 11. Paid ads (Search, LinkedIn, Meta)

**Why it’s included:** Only channel where you can **buy** predictable **sessions** to a landing page (still not “SEO,” fully measurable).

### What to include

- **Single landing page** per ad group (tool or guide).  
- **Ad copy** matches **headline on page** (message match).  
- **Disclaimer** in footer where needed (not legal/tax advice).  
- **Conversion event** in GA4 (scroll depth, tool start, newsletter signup).

### Cadence / how often

| Type | Suggested use |
|------|----------------|
| **Google Search (high-intent queries)** | **Always-on small budget** test (e.g. narrow keywords) **or** seasonal bursts (September intake, January insurance). Review **weekly** at start, then biweekly. |
| **LinkedIn (B2B mobility/HR)** | **2–4 week campaigns** around specific assets; **pause** if CPC doesn’t convert. |
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram)** | Use for **broad awareness sparingly**; better when you have a **simple hook** (checklist PDF, quiz-style tool). **Monthly** experiment budget unless ROAS proves out. |

### Measure

Cost per click, cost per **meaningful** session (30s+ or tool engagement), not vanity impressions.

## 12. Journalist & HARO-style responses

**Why it’s included:** When you’re quoted, the **article** drives **referral traffic** and **credibility**; readers click through to “see who said this.”

### What to include

- **2–3 sentence** quotable insight + **offer** to share a **link** to a relevant guide (many journalists won’t include it—still worth offering once).  
- **Bio line** with site URL for attribution.  
- **Fast response** within hours for HARO-style emails.

### Cadence / how often

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Scan requests** | **Daily** (5 min) if using HARO/Qwoted filters for Netherlands, expat, visa, EU mobility. |
| **Quality pitches sent** | **2–8 per month** is realistic; most won’t land—**one good hit** pays for the habit. |

### Measure

Backlinks + referral traffic from news domains; set Google Alerts for your name + brand.

## Summary: suggested weekly rhythm (example)

Not all channels every day—this is a **combined ceiling** for a small team:

| Block | Time | Channels |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Mon** | 45 min | LinkedIn post + comment rounds; scan HARO filter |
| **Tue** | 30 min | X/Threads post with link; Reddit check (no link unless fit) |
| **Wed** | 45 min | Newsletter draft or send; engage Facebook groups without links |
| **Thu** | 30 min | LinkedIn second post or comment focus; podcast outreach 2 pitches |
| **Fri** | 30 min | Review GA4 referrals + UTMs; plan next week’s **one hero URL** |

**Monthly:** YouTube or event if scheduled; paid campaign review; cut underperforming UTMs.
