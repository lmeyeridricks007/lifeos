## 1. Principles and risks

- **Group rules are law.** Pin posts and “About” often say **no self-promotion**, **no links**, or **admin approval**. Breaking them gets you removed and damages the brand.
- **Housing groups** are full of **scams** and tense members. Your job is **not** to look like a scammer: no “DM me,” no shortened mystery links, no pressure.
- **Participation first:** Answer **5–10** questions helpfully **without** your site before you ever drop a link (per group), unless a mod explicitly welcomes resources.
- **One link per thread** when allowed—and only if the page **directly answers** the question (BSN, visa orientation, checklist—not homepage).
- **Disclose:** e.g. “I run Expat Copilot (editorial guides, not legal advice)” the **first time** you link in a group.
- **Facebook Pages** vs **Groups:** You can **share** a post from your Page **into** a group only if rules allow; many groups disable it or treat it as spam. Prefer **personal profile** comments unless you’re posting as the Page with mod approval.

**UTM for Facebook (paste on your links):**  
`?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=group&utm_campaign=GROUP_SLUG`  
Example: `...&utm_campaign=expats-rotterdam`

## 2. Before you post checklist

### Step 1 — Profile credibility (5 minutes)

1. **Profile photo** visible (not a logo-only account if you’re commenting as a person).  
2. **Bio / Intro** or **Featured** on your profile: short line that you write NL relocation guides + link to expatcopilot.com **once**.  
3. **Cover photo** optional: calm, not aggressive marketing.

### Step 2 — Join the group

1. Search the group name on Facebook or open the URL from [Appendix A–D](#10-master-group-list-how-to-use-this-table).  
2. Click **Join**. Answer membership questions honestly (“living in NL / helping internationals with editorial resources”).  
3. If the link **404s** or the group is archived: search Facebook for the **same name**; update your local tracker.

### Step 3 — Read the rules

1. Open **Group rules** (left sidebar or pinned post).  
2. Screenshot or note: **links allowed?** **Promo days?** **Admin contact?**  
3. If rules say **no links**, plan to help with **text-only** answers and suggest they Google “Expat Copilot + topic” only if that’s still within rules—often **no**.

### Step 4 — Lurk 24–72 hours

1. Sort feed by **New posts**.  
2. Note tone: blunt? supportive? anti-landlord?  
3. Identify **admins** (badges on posts).

## 3. Step-by-step engagement workflow

### Phase A — Pure help (days 1–14 in a new group)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Answer **2–3 questions/week** in **comments**, text only: gemeente order, insurance timing, “you usually need BSN before…”, etc. |
| 2 | **Upvote** useful answers from others; build goodwill. |
| 3 | **Never** reply to housing scam posts with your link—report scam, move on. |

### Phase B — Link when it fits (after you’re a known voice)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Find a thread where someone asks for a **checklist**, **visa orientation**, **order of steps**, or **document prep**—not “rent my room.” |
| 2 | Write a **short** helpful answer in plain language **in the comment**. |
| 3 | Add **one** sentence: disclosure + **one** URL if rules allow. |
| 4 | If rules ban links: offer “happy to share the checklist I use—tell me if DM is OK” **only** if DMs are normal in that group; otherwise stop at text help. |

### Phase C — Optional admin outreach

1. **Message** an admin (or use “Contact admin”): short note that you have **free editorial** resources and ask **how** they prefer external links shared (weekly thread, wiki, never). See [§6](#6-admin-and-moderator-outreach).

## 4. Comment templates (no link)

Use these **without** any URL to stay safe in strict groups.

### Template A — Order of operations (arrival)

> In most cases people tackle it roughly in this order: address registration at the gemeente → BSN → basic health insurance (for many statuses) → bank account where the bank allows it. Exact order can depend on your permit and employer—IND and your gemeente pages are the official sources. Happy to clarify one step if you say your situation (student / highly skilled / EU).

### Template B — Insurance (non-legal)

> Basic (*basis*) health insurance is mandatory for most residents once you’re required to hold it—timing depends on your situation. The official insurers’ sites and **zorgwijzer.nl** are the neutral comparison starting points. If you’re not sure you’re liable yet, check the official conditions for your permit type (I’m not a broker—just went through this myself after moving from South Africa).

### Template C — Housing scam warning (no promo)

> Please be careful: if they ask for a deposit before you’ve seen the place or verified the landlord, it’s often a scam. Search the address and reverse-image search photos. Legit landlords usually expect viewing or formal contract.

### Template D — “I’m overwhelmed”

> Totally normal. Pick **one** next step for this week (often: gemeente appointment or BSN if you’re registered). Everything else queues behind that. You don’t have to solve it all in week one.

## 5. When you share a link (templates)

**Only** after rules allow and the URL **matches the question**.

### Template E — Single guide + disclosure

> I went through the same mess after moving from South Africa. I write **Expat Copilot** (editorial only—not legal advice). This page walks through **{{TOPIC}}** in one place:  
> {{URL_WITH_UTM}}  
> Hope it saves you a few rabbit holes. If something’s wrong, tell me and I’ll fix it.

**Example URL:**  
`https://www.expatcopilot.com/netherlands/municipality-registration-netherlands/?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=group&utm_campaign=expats-hague`

### Template F — Tool + soft CTA

> There’s a free **document readiness** checklist-style tool here (again: editorial, not legal advice):  
> {{URL_WITH_UTM}}  
> Use whatever your lawyer or school actually requires—this is just to organise your pile of papers.

### Template G — “Link in first comment” groups

Some admins allow links **only** in a follow-up comment:

1. Post **helpful text** in comment 1.  
2. Comment 2:  
   > Adding one link mods allow in comments: {{URL_WITH_UTM}} — my site Expat Copilot, same disclosure as above.

## 6. Admin and moderator outreach

Use **Messenger** to admins when you want a **stable** placement (pinned resources, wiki).

**Initial message to admin**

> Hi — I’m Lee. I moved from South Africa to the Netherlands and run **Expat Copilot**, free editorial guides/tools for NL arrival (not legal advice).  
>  
> I’m active in **{{GROUP_NAME}}** and want to respect your promo rules. Is there an approved way to share neutral resources—e.g. a weekly thread, a wiki link, or “never”—that you prefer?  
>  
> Example page: https://www.expatcopilot.com/netherlands/after-arriving-netherlands/  
>  
> Thanks for moderating a busy group.

**Follow-up (~10 days)**

> Hi again — floating my note in case it got lost. Happy to follow whatever format works for you.

**If they say “post on Sundays only” (example)**  
Log it in your tracker and **only** post then.

---

## 7. If someone replies in thread

- **Thank them** briefly.  
- **Don’t** paste three more links—one is enough.  
- If they ask a **legal** question: “A qualified immigration lawyer should confirm for your case—I can only speak to general orientation.”

## 8. Weekly rhythm and volume

| Activity | Suggested cap |
|----------|----------------|
| **Groups you actively monitor** | **5–12** total (not 50 shallowly) |
| **Helpful no-link comments** | **5–15 / week** across all groups |
| **Comments with your link** | **2–8 / week** total, only where allowed |
| **New group joins** | **1–2 / week** so you can actually lurk |

**Tier groups:** Spend most energy on **Appendix A** (general expat Q&A). Use **Appendix B** sparingly for links—often **text-only** scam warnings and empathy build more trust.

## 10. Master group list — how to use this table

- **Sources:** [DutchReview](https://dutchreview.com/culture/facebook-groups-for-internationals-in-major-dutch-cities/) (Oct 2021), [RentHunter](https://renthunter.nl/how-facebook-groups-help-you-find-housing-in-the-netherlands/) (Jan 2025), and public Facebook URLs from search.  
- **Verify before you rely on a link:** groups **merge, rename, go private, or disappear**. If a URL fails, search the **exact group name** inside Facebook while logged in.  
- **Engagement column:**  
  - **Primary** — good fit for **arrival / visa / life admin** questions; link sometimes allowed if rules permit.  
  - **Housing** — **listings** dominate; use **mostly** text help; link only when someone asks for **general** moving/registration context and rules allow.  
  - **Jobs** — link only when **relocation + work** intersects; never spam job threads.  
  - **Thematic** — nationality / women-only / publisher community; read **each** group’s rules.

**Duplicate names:** Facebook allows similarly named groups with different IDs; both may exist. We list **distinct URLs**; if two groups merged, remove the dead ID from your tracker.

**Per-group promotion column:** Appendices A–D include **Promote (bundles + city)**. Copy the **bundle path lists** from §10.1 into a spreadsheet column, then for each group note which bundles apply. In threads, still share **one** URL that matches the question.
