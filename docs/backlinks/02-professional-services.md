# Outreach targets: Professional services (relocation, law, tax, HR)

Companion to [Backlinks & promotion playbook](../backlinks-and-promotion.md) §2.2.

**Disclaimer:** Firms below are **examples of the type** of organisation to research—not a recommendation to spam them. **Verify** contacts on each company’s site. Prefer **relationships** and **guest value** over cold link begging.

**Concrete prospect list (relocation, law, tax, Big Four):** [07-concrete-outreach-prospects.md](./07-concrete-outreach-prospects.md) §D–F, **§L** (more relocation, recruitment, law).

## A. Relocation & global mobility (agencies / consultants)

| Organization (example type) | Resource / link page | Why a link fits | Who to contact | Example pitch hook |
|----------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **EURA (industry association)** | [eura-relocation.com](https://www.eura-relocation.com/) | Members are **exact** ICP; association sometimes lists **resources** or runs events | Membership / events team | “We’d like to propose a speaker topic + resource for your member newsletter.” |
| **Cartus / Aires / SIRVA** (RMCs) | Corporate **resources** or **blog** (search `site:cartus.com blog Netherlands` etc.) | Blog posts on **global mobility trends**; may cite external explainers | Content / marketing lead (LinkedIn) | “Data-light explainer on Dutch orientation year—we’ll cite your mobility report if you link the guide.” |
| **Local NL relocation boutiques** | Google: **relocation services Netherlands** → check each site’s **Resources** / **Blog** | Smaller sites often **want** quality outbound links and **guest posts** | Owner or marketing (usually on Contact page) | “We see your client FAQ stops at BSN—we have a deep guide; happy to co-brand a PDF.” |
| **Big Four mobility practices** (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG NL) | NL sites → **Global employer services** / **Immigration** | High authority; harder gate; need **substantive** angle | Partner or content team via contact form | “Employer checklist for highly skilled migrant timeline—attribute to us as supplementary reading.” |

**How to find 20 more:** LinkedIn search **“relocation manager” Netherlands**, note companies, then check **/blog** and **/resources**.

## B. Immigration law (law firms & lawyers)

Law sites vary: some have **“moving to the Netherlands”** hubs with external links; many only link to **IND**. Respect **bar rules** and **advertising**—pitch **educational** content.

| Organization (examples to research) | Resource / link page | Why a link fits | Who to contact | Example pitch hook |
|------------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Firms with “Knowledge” / “News” hubs** | Search: `immigration lawyer Netherlands blog` — open top 10 results | They publish **weekly** on visa changes; may link to **plain-language** companions | Marketing manager or named lawyer on byline | “Your post on [topic] is authoritative; we published a reader-facing timeline—link if it helps clients.” |
| **Legal500 / Chambers listed firms** | [Legal 500 Netherlands immigration](https://www.legal500.com/c/netherlands/immigration) (directory—use to **name** firms, then visit firm sites) | Firms invest in **SEO**; resource pages common | BD / marketing | “Non-competing glossary page we maintain—happy for you to fact-check one paragraph.” |

**Do not:** Offer money for links, reciprocal “link schemes,” or fake author bylines.

## Outreach templates

Replace placeholders; tailor **tone** to firm size (boutique = warmer; Big Four = shorter and factual).

**Placeholders:** `FIRM_NAME`, `THEIR_ARTICLE_OR_PAGE_URL`, `YOUR_PAGE_URL`, `YOUR_SITE_NAME`, `TOPIC`, `SPECIFIC_GAP`, `LAWYER_OR_AUTHOR_NAME` (if byline known).

### T1 — Relocation agency / boutique (resources or FAQ gap)

**Subject:** Idea to extend your client FAQ — TOPIC

```
Hi [Name / team],

I was on YOUR_ARTICLE_OR_PAGE_URL — your section on [what they cover well] is strong. Clients often still ask about SPECIFIC_GAP after that step.

We published YOUR_PAGE_URL as a free companion (we don’t sell relocation services). If it saves your team repeating the same explanation, would you consider linking it from that FAQ or a “Further reading” line?

If co-branding a one-pager PDF is easier for your client pack, we can discuss a simple attribution line.

Best,
[Your name]
[Your site / role]
[Email]
```

### T2 — Industry association (EURA-style) — event or newsletter

**Subject:** Speaker / resource idea for members — Netherlands arrival compliance

```
Dear [Events / membership team],

[FIRM_NAME] runs YOUR_SITE_NAME — editorial guides for people moving to the Netherlands (IND-aligned, non-legal advice).

We’d propose a 20-minute member briefing on TOPIC, or a short resource blurb for your newsletter linking to YOUR_PAGE_URL. No fee; goal is accurate information for mobility teams.

If you have a submission form or programme committee, happy to use that instead.

Kind regards,
[Your name]
[Email]
```

### T3 — Immigration law firm (after a specific blog post)

**Subject:** Reader-friendly supplement to your piece on TOPIC

```
Dear TEAM / LAWYER_OR_AUTHOR_NAME,

Your article YOUR_ARTICLE_OR_PAGE_URL is thorough for clients who want legal depth. We published a non-legal timeline for the same audience here: YOUR_PAGE_URL

If you think it helps readers who aren’t ready to instruct counsel yet, would you add it as “general information” with your usual disclaimer? We’re happy to add a line that your firm is the authority on legal advice.

Respectfully,
[Your name]
[YOUR_SITE_NAME — editorial only, not a law firm]
[Email]
```

### T4 — Tax / accounting firm (reciprocal editorial, soft ask)

**Subject:** Cross-link idea — first tax year in NL + your 30% ruling guide

```
Hi [Name],

We link to YOUR_ARTICLE_OR_PAGE_URL from our arrival hub because it explains [specific point] well.

We have YOUR_PAGE_URL for “first year in NL” order-of-operations (BSN, registration, when people typically involve an accountant). If it’s accurate from your perspective, would you consider a reciprocal “further reading” link? We’re open to your edits on the intro blurb.

Thanks,
[Your name]
[Email]
```

### T5 — HR association / employer branding / TechNL-style (webinar or PDF)

**Subject:** People teams — checklist for international hires (first 30 days in NL)

```
Hi [Programme / content contact],

International hires often stall on gemeente registration, BSN, and insurance before payroll can settle cleanly. We built YOUR_PAGE_URL as a neutral checklist for People teams (not legal advice).

Would this fit an upcoming webinar, Slack share for members, or “resources for HR” page? We can provide slides bullets and one canonical link.

Best,
[Your name]
[Organization]
[Email]
```

### T6 — Big Four / RMC (short, formal)

**Subject:** Supplementary client material — Netherlands HSM / orientation year timeline

```
Dear [Content / immigration practice contact],

We maintain YOUR_PAGE_URL — a client-facing timeline for TOPIC, citing IND. We’re not competing with advisory services; we use it to reduce basic FAQs.

If your Netherlands mobility pages list external references, we’d welcome consideration. Happy to align wording with your risk disclaimer.

Sincerely,
[Your name]
[Email]
```

### T7 — Follow-up (all B2B, 7–10 days)

**Subject:** Re: [original subject]

```
Hi [Name],

Brief follow-up on my email from [date]. If you’re not the right owner, a forward would help.

Either way, thanks for the work you do on YOUR_ARTICLE_OR_PAGE_URL.

[Your name]
```
